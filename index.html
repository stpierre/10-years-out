Lessons

FOSS can make careers

  • Experience without interview
  • Huge asset during job interviews
  • Literally millions of opportunities
  • Lots of companies are making lots of money with lots of different models:
    • Redhat: support and integration
    • Metacloud: operations as a service
    • Gitlab: hosting

Dev+Ops, not dev or ops

  • Temperature of the room: dev vs. ops
  • Everyone wants to hire experienced generalists, not specialists
  • You need to be able to write code and run systems. E.g.: Netflix
  • Cloud makes this all the more important from both sides
  • There are companies still doing waterfall, but you don't want to work for them.

The cloud, annoyingly, is everything

  • It's over-hyped, but also revolutionary
  • Everything is ephemeral
  • Automate all the things. Examples:
    • Ops: Duplicate 400-node, 60-role prod environment this afternoon
    • Eng: Entire dev environment is torn down and rebuilt every night, redeploy everything automatically
  • Semi-terrible semi-honest suggestion: get a job at Amazon

jobs.metacloud.com

:D

Follow interesting work

  • All the interesting stuff is happening on Linux, in the cloud, on bespoke applications
  • Join a startup (if you can stomach it):
    • Exciting technology
    • Aggressive benefits
    • (Often) aggressive pay
    • A ticket to the Internet Lottery
    • Long hours, high expectations
  • If not, join an established company looking for the same kinds of skills startups are looking for
  • Education gets your foot in the door
  • How to maximize the value of a liberal arts education?
  • It's an asset; make it something worth talking about by diversifying
  • Take opportunities for experience, especially interdisciplinary
  • Make it applicable to your attitude: a wholistic way of interrogating the world

Computer science for liberal arts

  • Internet of Things means we have more data than ever before
  • In the future, every problem will be a Big Data problem. E.g., Amplify assessments; gait analysis for physical therapy
  • LA: wholistic way of interrogating the world (e.g., everything is a text)
  • If LA is to remain a wholistic way of interrogating the world, it must include ways to interrogate petabytes of data
